Californian Wildfires & Climate Change

11 October 2020

How the 2020 wildfifres relate to climate change

Introduction

  The 2020 California Wildfires are a huge item in US News for about a month now. The fires have already killed 30+ people. My question: how do the wildfires relate to climate change?

A rapidly changing climate

  It's hard-if not impossible - to pinpoint the direct cause of the Californian Wildfires. But, news, social media and scientists relate the fires to climate change. I hope to clarify their reasoning a bit in this article. 

   While it is impossible to prove causation, I will try to prove climate change and elaborate on its meaning for the current wildfires - and those yet to come.

Heavier storms

  Meteorologist Eric Holthaus described how the world's climate is rapidly changing:

"It [Heavier storms] is the future normal".

It goes to show how the world's climate is in turmoil. Lesser rainfall in some areas, and tropical wheater in others can lead to disasters like the Californian wildfires.

Extremer temperatures

  Since 1850, the world is 1.1–1.3℃ hotter. But, the climate can swing way more drastically on the regional level: some winters get colder and most summers get much warmer.

Air pollution

 &enspNASA's climate department shows how carbon levels sky-rocketed and reached all-time-highs as early as 1950. Truly, it's staggering to watch.

With that amount of air pollution, it's no wonder that the Greenhouse effect is amplifying every year. It not only hurts the earth globally, but may also extremify wheather on the regional level (for instance in California).

More Vegetation

  Higher carbon dioxide levels, means that plants grow more easily. But, all that vegetation can burn too. In other words: more green means higher flames.

Drought

  In terms of the US, Carbonbrief reported an 'alarming increase in combined heatwaves and droughts'. Officials blame drought for the 2020 wildfires too; it had caused so many individual fires in Medecino National Forest, that a huge wildfire erupted.

Conclusion

With these points, I think it's rather hard to argue 'the Californian Wildfires have nothing to do with climate change'. We can't prove it, there's abundant evidence to correlate the event with the worsening of earth's climate-and to discover a pattern of more disasters the past & coming years. 
What it means is that we should truly work on climate change as we, increasingly, see its effects taking shape.

Act now, before it's too late
