Editorial: The people’s right

6 October 2020

Our civil rights on nature

Introduction

  All Scandinavian countries (Iceland, Norway, Denmark, Finland, Sweden) have a law called ‘The People’s Right’. It means you can eat berries and other products of nature – for free. In other words, these governments return people their ‘right’ to nature.

  Now, this got me thinking: along the same line of ‘The People’s Right’ you could argue governments hsould do anything they can to prevent huge emissions and rear environmental projects.

Murmurred voices

  Governments have grown so powerful in the past decenia: populations increased fast and voices are getting murmurred out by the masses. But, these leaders should never forget it is each individual that gave up power for them to govern: they should protect people from dark futures and illegal pollution levels at all costs.

Legal Power

  This week we talk about the violation and weakening of the NEPA law. It means that small communities can’t raise their voices so well against the US government: any public health, environmental or religious concern is silenced and has less standing in court.

Conclusion

  I think good governments should honor people just like wild nature. As our numbers grow and voices dim in the murmer we should be careful to maintain our civil rights. In animal terms: would we rather be wild or live in a zoo?
