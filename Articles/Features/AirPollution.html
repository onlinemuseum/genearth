Air Pollution

8 July 2020

Air pollution is among the most debated forms of pollution. Let us consider its impact.

  Some people categorize pollution in 11 segments.

  1. Air
  2. Electromagnetic
  3. Light
  4. Littering
  5. Noise
  6. Plastic
  7. Soil
  8. Radioactivity
  9. Thermal
  10. Water
  11. Visual

We shall walk through each of these segments on a weekly basis, and at the end come up with additions to this list: for instance, deforestation or ecological disruption may be worth adding. This time, we address air pollution.

Harmful gas

   The air is made up of gas and small particles that we breathe in every day. Normally there is nothing wrong with that, but when certain elements reach high concentrations, they can form a problem to human health or impact the workings of nature.

   Some harmful air pollutants are carbon monoxide, nitrogen dioxide and ozone. These are usually dispersed by humans and industry.

Factory workers may develop respiratory problems by inhaling dust particles or harmful gasses.

Effects

  Air pollution may increase mortality rates and the risk of having diseases. It has already formed sizeable problems in metropoles such as Beijing and New York. Smog can reduce visibilty on windstill days, reduce sunlight and vitamin D, cause astma and form a health issue at large. Moreover, smog in the form of ozone causes the well-known green-house effect, which makes the earth warm up.

In certain cities smog is becoming a notable problem

Conclusion

   All in all, air pollution is a serious item on our list of pollutants. It has rightly been monitored and regulated by governments. But, there is - without a doubt - room for improvement. There is, for one, much left to be desired in countries that care less for human health and more for economic prospeperity.

Appendix: Extra informetion

Some extra info about three common air pollutants. You may have heard of these gasses:

  • Carbon Monoxide The first of the three examples is one of the most common air pollutants. People may experience nausea and fatigue, while not realizing it is the invisible gas that’s causing symptoms.

  • Nitrogen Dioxide NO2 mostly derives from fossil-fueled engines. It can cause lung diseases if people are commonly exposed to the gas – by smoking cigarettes or working in certain heavy industries. Nitrogen Dioxide furthermore creates Ozone, which we’ll discuss next.

  • Ozone In some ways, Ozone protects the earth: it decreases incomin UV light from the sun. Yet, in other ways it can be quite harmful. Among other things, the gas can cause cardiovascular and respiratory problems. Another harmful effect of Ozone is the “green house effect”: after sunlight reflects on the earth’s surface ozone traps it inside the atmosphere, causing earth to warm up.
