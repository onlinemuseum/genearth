The Sunda Island Tiger

2 October 2020

Indonesia's indegenous tiger species

Introduction

  The Sunda island tiger is on the WWF list of ‘critically endangered species’. It used to live in several parts of the Indonesian Sunda island. But, it only lives on the island of Sumatra now. The Javan tiger and the Bali tiger are both considered Sunda island tigers, but both those tiger populations are now wholly extinct. That only leaves us witht the Sumatran tiger (the name that’s most commonly used).

A Sumatran tiger in the wild. Photo by Rebecca Campbell on Unsplash

  There are less than 400 Sumatran tigers left in the wild and they suffer from:

  • Habitat loss (Sumatra lost half of its forest in the past few years)
  • Poaching and hunting (to protect livestock)
  • Illegal wildlife trade (the notorious tiger farms)
