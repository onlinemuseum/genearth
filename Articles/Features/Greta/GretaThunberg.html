Greta Thunberg's speech

6 October 2020

A message that makes you think

Introduction

  All Scandinavian countries (Iceland, Norway, Denmark, Finland, Sweden) have a law called ‘The People’s Right’. It means you can eat berries and other products of nature – for free. In other words, these governments return people their ‘right’ to nature.

  Back in 2019, Greta Thunberg joined the UN Climate Action Summit. Her speech there was quite bold and unexpected, but certainly makes you think.


The flag of the UN

Bold Stance

  When Greta said ‘How dare you’ a sudden silence befell the room: everybody thought her words would praise the UN’s actions. Instead, she directs herself against the UN and ‘the old establishment’

First Thoughts

  At first I was shocked and found her tone a bit too aggressive. But, the more I rewatch this clip, the more I appreciate Greta’s stance. We, the UN and even the UN’s Climate department allow emission, pollution and deforestation numbers to grow every year, but still we don’t do anything significant. We allow greed and passivity to stand in the way of a degrading earth.

Conclusion

   You don’t have to agree with me, but Greta’s speech certainly makes you think. So, why not give it a watch?
